__Graphene-Based Terahertz Detectors and Emitters__.
I am working on a senior honors thesis in graphene P-I-N junction terahertz detectors. The terahertz frequency range of the electromagnetic spectrum (sub-mm to mm wavelengths) has tremendous potential for applications in security, spectroscopy, astronomy, medicine, and more. Yet, this range can only studied with expensive machines that operate at cryogenic temperatures. Graphene has shown great experimental and theoretical promise for sustaining optoelectronic devices that operate in the THz range. Furthermore, graphene is wholly integrable with silicon electronics, cheap to produce (compared to the existing types of THz electronics), and has the potential to support the development of devices that operate at room-temperature. My senior thesis is to develop a type of graphene THz detector that can operate at high temperatures with a high responsivity, primarily by exploiting the hot-electron photo-thermoelectric effect and plasmonic resonance effect in graphene. I have also written a proposal for the __[National Science Foundation's Graduate Research Fellowship](https://www.nsfgrfp.org/)__ that focuses on a similar optoelectronic phenomenon in graphene: terahertz lasing.
